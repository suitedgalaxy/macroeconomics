The Circular Flow Model

Factor Payments- payment for the factors of production: rent, wages, interest, and profit.
Transfer Payments- the government redistributes income (e.g. welfare, social security).
Subsidies- government payments to businesses.

The Production Possibilities Curve and The Business Cycle

The production possibilities curve shows the trade-offs in the ways an economy can allocate its resources. It graphically demonstrates scarcity
trade-offs, opportunity costs, and efficiency. Any point along the curve represents a possible combination that can be produced given a certain
productivity or efficiency. The curve is not fixed — an increase in efficiency or productivity will shift the curve outward and vice versa.

Unemployment

Unemployment- workers that are actively looking for a job but aren't working.
The Unemployment Rate- the percentage of people in the labor force who want a job but are not working.


Three Types of Unemployment
  1. Frictional Unemployment
      2. Temporary unemployment or being between jobs. Individuals are qualified workers with transferable skills.
      Seasonal Unemployment is a specific type of frictional unemployment, which is due to time of year and the nature of the job.

  2. Structural Unemployment
      3. Changes in the labor force makes some skills obsolete. These workers do not have transferable skills and these jobs will
      never come back. Workers must learn new skills to get a job. The permanent loss of these jobs is called "creative destruction."
      Technological Unemployment is a type of structural unemployment where automation and machinery replace workers.

  3. Cyclical Unemployment
      4. Unemployment caused by a recession. This is sometimes called "demand deficient unemployment."

Frictional and structural unemployment are present at all times because some people will always be between jobs or replaced by technology.
The Natural Rate of Unemployment is frictional unemployment plus structural unemployment. This is the amount of unemployment when
the economy is healthy and growing. We calculate GDP at Full Employment Output, or at the natural rate of unenployment — which excludes
cyclical unemployment — and label it Y.

Criticisms of the Unemployment Rate
Discouraged Workers- some people are no longer looking for a job because they have given up (they no longer count in the labor force thus
the unemployment rate falls).
Underemployed Workers- someone who wants more hours but can't get them is still considered employed.
Inequalities- the overall unemployment rate doesn't show disparity for minorities and teenagers.

Gross Domestic Product (GDP)

GDP is the dollar value of all final goods and services produced within a country in one year. Final means that GDP
only counts new goods and services.


GDP Per Capita is GDP divided by the population. It identifies on average how many products each person makes. GDP per capita is the
best measure of a nation's standard of living
